Buying Checklist

Big Rock #1: Only buy stocks in a market uptrend

Market in confirmed uptrend

Buying stocks during a market uptrend means investing when the overall market is rising, which typically provides a favorable environment for stock gains.

Big Rock #2: Focus on companies with big earnings growth and a new, innovative product or service

EPS growth 25% or higher in recent quarters

Earnings per Share (EPS) growth reflects a company's profitability. A high growth rate indicates the company is generating more profit per share, which is a positive sign for investors.

Composite Rating of 95 or higher

Combines various fundamental and technical factors into one score. A high rating suggests the company excels in multiple key areas, making it a strong overall performer.

EPS Rating of 80 or higher

Evaluates a company's earnings growth over the past five years. A high rating indicates consistent and strong earnings growth, which is attractive to investors.

Average Annual EPS growth 25% or more over last 3 years

Shows the average yearly growth in EPS over the past three years, highlighting companies with sustained profitability and growth.

Sales growth 20%-25% or higher in most recent quarter

Indicates increasing demand for a company's products or services, which is a positive indicator of business health and potential future growth.

Return on equity (ROE) of 17% or higher

Measures how effectively a company uses shareholders' equity to generate profit. A high ROE indicates efficient management and strong financial performance.

SMR Rating (Sales + Margins + Return on Equity) of A or B

The SMR Rating combines sales growth, profit margins, and return on equity into one measure. A high rating signifies a company performing well across these key metrics.

Accelerating earnings growth

Company's earnings are increasing at a faster rate over time, indicating robust financial health and potential for future gains.

Big Rock #3: Buy stocks being heavily bought by mutual funds

Increase in number of funds that own the stock in recent quarters

An increase in the number of mutual funds owning a stock suggests institutional confidence and can drive the stock's price higher.

Accumulation/Distribution Rating of A, B or C

Indicates the balance of buying and selling of a stock. A high rating shows more accumulation (buying) than distribution (selling), suggesting demand for the stock.

Relative Strength Rating of 80 or higher

Compares a stock's price performance to other stocks. A high rating indicates that the stock is outperforming most other stocks in the market.

Share price above $15

Stocks with share prices above $15 are generally more stable and less volatile than lower-priced stocks, making them safer investments.

Average daily volume of 400,000 shares or more

Indicates strong liquidity, making it easier to buy and sell shares without affecting the stock price significantly.

Chart Analysis: Buy stocks as they break out of the common patterns that launch big moves

Breaking out of sound base or alternative buy point

Buying stocks as they break out of a sound base or alternative buy point can capitalize on momentum, leading to significant gains as the stock continues to rise.

Volume at least 40% to 50% above average on breakout

Higher-than-average volume during a breakout indicates strong interest and buying pressure, which can drive the stock price higher.

Relative strength line in new high ground

Compares the stock's performance to the market. A new high in this line indicates the stock is outperforming the market, signaling strength.

Within 5% of ideal buy point

Buying within 5% of the ideal buy point ensures that you are entering the stock at a favorable price, maximizing potential gains while minimizing risk.

Types of Options
